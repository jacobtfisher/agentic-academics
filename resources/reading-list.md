# Reading List & Resources

Curated links for academics getting started with agentic AI tools.

## Official Documentation

- [Claude Code Documentation](https://docs.anthropic.com/en/docs/claude-code) — comprehensive guide to features, setup, and configuration
- [Claude Code GitHub Repository](https://github.com/anthropics/claude-code) — source code, issues, community discussion
- [Model Context Protocol (MCP) Specification](https://modelcontextprotocol.io/) — technical documentation for MCP

## Courses

- [Anthropic Skilljar Courses](https://anthropic.skilljar.com/) — structured courses on prompting, tool use, and building with Claude
- [Prompt Engineering Guide](https://docs.anthropic.com/en/docs/build-with-claude/prompt-engineering/overview) — Anthropic's official prompting best practices

## Articles on AI and Academic Research

### The Big Picture

- **Hall, A. (2026). "The 100x Research Institution."** *Free Systems (Substack)*. [Link](https://freesystems.substack.com/p/the-100x-research-institution). A vision for how AI agents could transform research institutions — living research, automated replication, research swarms.

- **Messing, S. & Tucker, J. A. (2026). "The Train Has Left the Station: Agentic AI and the Future of Social Science Research."** *Brookings Institution*. [Link](https://www.brookings.edu/articles/the-train-has-left-the-station-agentic-ai-and-the-future-of-social-science-research/). Institutional implications of agentic AI for social science — submission surges, peer review strain, equity concerns.

### The Human Side

- **Rosenfeld, A. (2026). "Why I May 'Hire' AI Instead of a Graduate Student."** *Science, 391*(6790). [Link](https://www.science.org/content/article/why-i-may-hire-ai-instead-graduate-student). A professor's honest reckoning with the temptation to use AI over mentoring a novice student.

- **Kotran, A. (2025). "Writing is thinking. What about co-writing with AI?"** *Free Systems (Substack)*. [Link](https://alexkotran.substack.com/p/writing-is-thinking-so-should-kids). On desirable difficulties, the generation-inspection-revision loop, and why productive struggle matters.

### Learning Theory

- **Bjork, R. A., & Bjork, E. L. (2011).** Making things hard on yourself, but in a good way: Creating desirable difficulties to enhance learning. *Psychology and the Real World*, 56–64.

## MCP Servers for Academics

- [zotero-mcp](https://github.com/anthropics/zotero-mcp) — connect Claude Code to your Zotero reference library
- [Scholar Gateway](https://docs.scholargateway.ai/) — Wiley's MCP connector for semantic search across 3M+ peer-reviewed articles; works with Claude Pro via OAuth
- [paper-search-mcp](https://github.com/openags/paper-search-mcp) — unified search across 20+ academic databases (CrossRef, OpenAlex, Semantic Scholar, arXiv, PubMed, SSRN, and more)
- [arxiv-mcp-server](https://github.com/blazickjp/arxiv-mcp-server) — search, download, and store arXiv preprints locally
- [Semantic Scholar MCP](https://github.com/zongmin-yu/semantic-scholar-fastmcp-mcp-server) — paper search, citation networks, and recommendations via the Semantic Scholar API
- [Obsidian MCP](https://github.com/MarkusPfworked/obsidian-mcp) — read/write/search an Obsidian vault (check for current maintained versions)
